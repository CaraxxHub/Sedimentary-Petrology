Lithology & Framework Grains

Mastering the identification and classification of quartz, feldspars, and lithic fragments using the Gazzi-Dickinson method

üéØ Learning Objectives

  • Understand the QFL classification system and its components
  • Apply the Gazzi-Dickinson point-counting method
  • Identify and classify quartz varieties (monocrystalline vs. polycrystalline)
  • Distinguish K-feldspar from plagioclase and recognize alteration features
  • Classify lithic fragments by texture and origin (volcanic, metamorphic, sedimentary)
  • Calculate and interpret the Metamorphic Index (MI)

1. The QFL Classification System

The simplest and most robust sandstone nomenclature characterizes sands by an adjective reflecting the relative abundance of their three main components: Quartz (Q), Feldspars (F), and Lithic fragments (L).

üîë Key Principle: Gazzi-Dickinson Method

The Gazzi-Dickinson point-counting method removes effects related to breakage of coarse-grained rock fragments into their monomineralic constituents. Crystals >62.5 Œºm within rock fragments are counted as the mineral (Q or F), not as the lithic fragment. This provides a more transport-invariant measure of sediment composition.

Main components exceeding 10% QFL are listed in order of abundance. For example:

  • Quartzo-feldspatho-lithic sand: L > F > Q > 10% QFL
  • Feldspatho-lithic sand: L > F > 10% QFL > Q
  • Litho-quartzose sand: Q > L > 10% QFL > F

Statistical Requirements

For statistically robust representation of eroded lithologies and provenance mixing, a minimum of 300 framework grains is required, with 500+ grains being ideal for detailed provenance analysis.

2. Quartz Classification

Quartz is divided into monocrystalline (Qm) and polycrystalline (Qp) varieties, with important provenance implications:

Quartz TypeCharacteristicsProvenance Implications
Qm (Monocrystalline)Single crystal, may show undulatory extinctionPlutonic, volcanic, or recycled sedimentary sources
Qp (Polycrystalline)Aggregate of interlocking crystalsMetamorphic or plutonic sources
Qp with tectonic fabricElongated crystals, sutured boundaries, preferred orientationMetamorphic sources (mylonites, schists)
Qp without tectonic fabricEquant crystals, no preferred orientationPlutonic or vein quartz

In the Gazzi-Dickinson method, quartz crystals >62.5 Œºm within rock fragments (plutonic, metamorphic, or sedimentary) are counted separately as "Quartz in r.f." but contribute to total quartz, not to lithic fragments.

3. Feldspar Classification

Feldspars (F = K + P) are subdivided into K-feldspar (orthoclase, microcline, sanidine) and plagioclase (albite to anorthite series):

Feldspar TypeOptical CharacteristicsCommon Sources
K-feldsparCross-hatch twinning (microcline), simple Carlsbad twins, perthitic textureGranites, gneisses, arkoses
PlagioclasePolysynthetic (albite) twinning, zoning commonPlutonic, volcanic, metamorphic rocks
Fresh feldsparClear, well-defined twinsFirst-cycle sediment, arid climate
Altered feldsparSericite, clay minerals, vacuoles, cloudy appearanceWeathered or diagenetically modified

The P/F Ratio

The plagioclase/feldspar ratio (P/F) provides important provenance information:

  • Low P/F (~0.2‚Äì0.4): Granitic/cratonic sources
  • Intermediate P/F (~0.5‚Äì0.6): Mixed plutonic sources
  • High P/F (~0.7‚Äì0.9): Volcanic arc or mafic plutonic sources

4. Lithic Fragment Classification

Lithic fragments (L = Lv + Lm + Ls) are aphanitic rock fragments <62.5 Œºm grain size. They provide the most direct and richest provenance information.

Volcanic Lithics (Lv)

TextureDescriptionSource Rock

MI = Œ£(Rank √ó Proportion of that rank) / Total metamorphic lithics. An MI of 3.5 indicates medium-grade metamorphic sources, while MI >4 suggests high-grade metamorphic sources with significant gneissic/amphibolite contributions. MI tracks unroofing trends in collisional orogens.

Sedimentary Lithics (Ls)

  • Carbonate extrabasinal (CE): Limestone, dolostone fragments (micritic, sparitic, biomicritic)
  • Siliciclastic: Siltstone, shale, chert fragments
  • Intrabasinal (CI): Contemporaneous bioclasts, peloids, glauconite
